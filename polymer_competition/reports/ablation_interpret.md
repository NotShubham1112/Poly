Generated by `ablate.py` against the available OOF predictions.

## What this measures

`ablate.py` drops one model from the OOF matrix at a time and re-runs the
SLSQP weight optimizer. `delta_r2` is the change in optimized-weights R² on
the OOF rows; negative numbers mean dropping the model *hurts* the ensemble,
so the model is contributing signal.

Sorted descending (largest positive / smallest negative at top).

## What this tells us about the v28 plan

The plan's estimated gains assumed all 7 v28 changes would help, but the
ablations show three things:

1. **Not every model helps.** RF and Ridge are zero-contribution. Multitask
   won't help until it's properly trained (5-epoch OOFs are noise). On egc,
   four of eight models are zero-contribution.

2. **One model dominates on TG.** MLP carries 37% of the weight. Any TG
   improvement strategy should start with making MLP stronger (better
   features, longer training, target transform).

3. **EGC ensemble is redundant.** With 4 dead models and 4 active ones,
   pruning is free. Once v28 egc features ship, re-run this ablation —
   the dead models will likely move to active and the score will improve.

## Next actions, ranked by measured impact

1. **Re-run ablation after multitask training completes.** The current
   multitask delta (0.0000) is because it has 5-epoch OOFs and no test
   pickles; once the 80-epoch run finishes, expect it to enter the top 3.
2. **Prune dead models from the submission pipeline** (rf, ridge on tg;
   gat, gcn, rf, mpnn on egc). Free compute, no score change.
3. **Improve MLP.** Its -0.0059 drop signal on TG and -0.0019 on EGC is the
   largest single-model lever. Try (a) target transform consistency, (b)
   longer training with early stopping, (c) feature interactions.
4. **Train v28 egc models.** The current submission for egc uses v27 OOFs
   because v28 egc has no completed runs. Once they exist, rerun
   `ablate.py --target egc --exp_ver v28`.
